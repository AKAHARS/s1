Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known celestial body to support life. It has a unique combination of atmosphere, water, land, and climate that allows diverse ecosystems to thrive. About 71% of Earth's surface is covered by water, primarily oceans, while the remaining 29% consists of continents and islands.

Earth is the fifth-largest planet in the solar system, with an equatorial diameter of about 12,756 kilometers. Its shape is not a perfect sphere but an oblate spheroid, slightly flattened at the poles and bulging at the equator due to its rotation. The total surface area of Earth is approximately 510 million square kilometers, with about 71% covered by water and 29% by land. Earth’s mass is around 5.97 × 10²⁴ kilograms, and it has a circumference of roughly 40,075 kilometers at the equator. This size and structure support a stable atmosphere, diverse ecosystems, and a dynamic climate, making it uniquely suited for life.

he radius of Earth is an important measure that helps define the planet's overall size and shape. On average, Earth's radius is about 6,371 kilometers (3,959 miles). However, because Earth is not a perfect sphere but an oblate spheroid, its radius varies slightly. The equatorial radius is approximately 6,378 kilometers, while the polar radius is about 6,357 kilometers, showing a slight flattening at the poles. This difference is caused by the Earth's rotation, which causes the planet to bulge at the equator. Understanding Earth's radius is crucial for calculating its surface area, volume, gravity, and satellite orbits.
